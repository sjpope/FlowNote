Topic: History - The Industrial Revolution

Topic: Literature - American Realism
Content:
American Realism was a style of writing that emerged in the late 19th century around the time of the Civil War and lasted until the early 20th century. It was characterized by a straightforward, unidealized portrayal of life—a departure from the more romanticized narratives popular at the time. Realist authors like Mark Twain, Henry James, and Edith Wharton sought to write about everyday activities and experiences, without romantic embellishment or excessive emotionalism. Their work focuses on middle-class characters and scenarios, depicting life as it truly was.

Keywords:
American Realism, Mark Twain, Henry James, Edith Wharton, 19th Century

Summary:
American Realism, flourishing around the Civil War, focuses on depicting everyday life with straightforward and unromanticized narratives.

Autocomplete Example:
Q: Notable American Realist authors include...
A: Mark Twain, Henry James, and Edith Wharton.
